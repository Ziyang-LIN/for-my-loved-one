“Stair is foul, and foul is stair.”

Location: Siteless
Date: 2023
Type: Individual Project
Supervised by: Ercument Gorgul, Faye Song

PROJECT BRIEF

The Macbeth stage design, situated within an expansive park or open area, challenges traditional theatrical conventions by emphasizing integration with nature and fostering a novel, immersive audience experience. This portable stage is conceived as a transformative spatial entity: its structural components use the fundamental ‘stair’ to reassemble and simulate various architectural elements such as doors, columns, and roofs.

In essence, the design hinges on the versatility of a singular building block —the stair— that can be endlessly manipulated. This adaptability ensures that the stage’s appearance can differ for individual Acts and Scenes, introducing a dynamic variability between performances. The stage’s foundation on a rotating plate further augments its spatial dynamism, granting spectators diverse viewing perspectives. Additionally, the chosen construction material is optimized for projections, facilitating seamless integration of visuals and sceneries onto the structure itself.

This design concept, by bridging performance and environment, posits a visionary approach to theatre. It not only encourages flexibility in stage presentation but also promotes a harmonious interplay between man-made structures and the natural world, redefining audience experience within an architectural framework.
